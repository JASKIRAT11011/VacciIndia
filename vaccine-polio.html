Polio Vaccine

Polio vaccines are vaccines used to prevent poliomyelitis (polio).

Children get polio vaccine to protect against polio, or poliomyelitis.
Polio vaccine is of two types :
IPV is given by shot in the leg or arm, depending on the patientâ€™s age.
OPV (Oral polio vaccine) is liquid vaccine drops.
Oral polio vaccine (OPV)
The OPV is a live but weakened form of the virus which makes the body produce antibodies against it without developing into the disease. Given as oral drops, it protects not only the person who has taken them but also others living around her.
Inactivated polio vaccine (IPV)
The IPV, on the other hand, while highly effective, only protects the vaccinated person, and not others. It is given by injection.
This vaccine has recently started being given in fractional doses. India is the first country to introduce fractional doses of the IPV as a way of protecting babies from polio during the current global IPV shortage.
We recommend that children get four doses of polio vaccine. They should get one dose at each of the following ages: 2 months old, 4 months old, 6 through 18 months old, and 3 through 5 years old.

The polio vaccine protects your child against polio or poliomyelitis, which is a highly infectious viral disease spread through the faeces of infected people.
Polio vaccine is Important

  1. Polio mainly affects children under the age of five. Older people with lower immunity levels are also vulnerable.
  2. Polio virus can rapidly lead to paralysis and even death.
  3. There is no cure for polio. The most effective way of preventing polio is by immunising your child with the polio vaccine at the right time.

The Polio vaccine should not be given if your baby has any severe, life-threatening allergies.
The Polio vaccine can be delayed if your baby:

  1. is not feeling well.
  2. a mild illness, such as a cold
  3. has fever

Polio is a disease caused by a virus. People have feared polio for hundreds of years because

  1. It can paralyze arm, leg, and breathing muscles.
  2. The polio virus enters through the mouth and ends up in the nervous system.
  3. It can rapidly lead to paralysis and even death.
